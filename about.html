ABOUT ME

My Story

My name is Muhammad Subhan, and I am an IGCSE student based in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. While many people know me as someone who enjoys aviation, what truly drives me is something broader: a desire to understand how complex systems work, why they work the way they do, and how they can continually be improved.

That curiosity has shaped the way I approach almost everything I do. Whether I am studying for school, attending an industry event, completing an online certification, researching a topic that interests me, or building this website, I rarely stop after finding the answer to a question. I usually find myself asking another question, then another, until I understand not only what something is, but why it exists and how it fits into the bigger picture.

01

Why Aviation?

Aviation became the place where all of my interests naturally came together.

To many people, aviation begins and ends with an aircraft taking off and landing. For me, that is only the visible outcome of thousands of people, technologies, decisions, and processes working together with extraordinary precision.

Every flight represents engineering, operations, maintenance, meteorology, navigation, communication, safety, leadership, and teamwork, all operating to an incredibly high standard. That level of coordination is something I have always admired.

My interest has never been limited to the cockpit alone. While becoming a commercial pilot is my long-term goal, I also want to understand the wider industry surrounding aviation.

I enjoy learning about aircraft systems, airport operations, airline management, flight planning, aviation safety, emerging technologies, and the innovations shaping the future of global air travel.

The simplest answer is that aviation never stopped challenging me.

Many interests fade with time. Mine became stronger. The more I learned about aircraft, the more I wanted to understand the systems behind them. Learning about systems led me to operations. Operations led me to airports. Airports led me to airline strategy, safety, engineering, and the decisions that allow millions of passengers to travel safely every day.

Instead of satisfying my curiosity, every answer created new questions. That is why aviation has remained such a significant part of my life.

02

How I Think

I believe curiosity is one of the most valuable qualities a person can develop. It is easy to memorise information. It is much harder, and much more rewarding, to understand why something works.

Whenever I study a new topic, I naturally find myself exploring beyond what is required. I enjoy analysing systems, identifying connections between ideas, and understanding the reasoning behind decisions.

That mindset influences the way I learn, solve problems, and approach challenges. I prefer understanding the full picture rather than memorising isolated facts.

03

Continuous Learning

Outside school, I actively look for opportunities to continue learning. I complete online courses, attend conferences and industry events whenever possible, follow developments in aviation, and build personal projects that allow me to apply what I have learned.

Every experience adds another piece to my understanding and reminds me that meaningful progress comes from consistent effort rather than occasional moments of motivation.

I have learned that growth is rarely dramatic. More often, it is built through small improvements repeated over long periods of time.

I try to become a little better each day, whether by learning a new concept, improving an existing skill, or challenging myself to think differently. I do not expect success to happen overnight, and I do not believe there is a shortcut to genuine achievement.

04

The Professional I Want to Become

The qualities I try to develop extend beyond technical knowledge. Aviation is an industry built on professionalism, discipline, communication, teamwork, responsibility, and sound judgement.

These are not qualities that suddenly appear when someone begins flying professionally. They are habits developed long before that point.

I place just as much importance on building my character as I do on building my knowledge. I want to become someone who approaches responsibility calmly, communicates clearly, works effectively with others, and never stops learning.

05

Looking Ahead

My long-term goal is clear: I want to become a commercial pilot.

However, I hope that my career will represent more than a job title. I want to be recognised as someone who approaches every responsibility with professionalism, remains calm under pressure, continues learning, and consistently strives to improve.

I want to contribute positively to every organisation I become part of, not only through technical competence but also through integrity, teamwork, and a willingness to help others succeed.

I know that I am still at the beginning of my journey. There is still an enormous amount to learn, countless experiences to gain, and many challenges to overcome.

Rather than seeing that as intimidating, I find it exciting. Every milestone ahead represents another opportunity to grow, develop new skills, and become a better version of myself.

06

Why This Website Exists

This website is not intended to present a finished story or suggest that I have already achieved my ambitions.

It documents the beginning of a journey that I hope will continue for decades. Every certification, event, experience, and achievement included here represents another step forward.

As I continue learning and progressing, this portfolio will grow alongside me.

If there is one idea I hope visitors remember after leaving this page, it is not simply that I want to become a pilot.

I hope they remember someone who is genuinely curious, committed to continuous improvement, willing to put in the work, and determined to earn every opportunity.

Becoming a pilot is not the destination. It is the responsibility that I am working every day to become worthy of.
